Problem 1

They are packaged in plastic bottles.

And since only 8% of plastic is actually recycled and plastic never really decomposes, plastic bottles don’t seem very ‘eco-friendly’.

Problem 2

Dish Soap is about 90% water

Which means we are basically shipping water around the country - burning carbon - when virtually everyone has water in their home.

Problem 3

Made with SLS to create artificial suds.

Most Dish Soaps - even top ‘eco brands’ like 7th Generation - contain SLS, which doesn’t ‘clean’ your dishes, it just makes more suds. According to the David Suzuki Foundation SLS is “… toxic to aquatic organisms.”
